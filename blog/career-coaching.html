CAREER COACHING

May 6, 2021 |

Types of coaching

Coaching is a solution-oriented approach, where a coach works with a client to explore what concrete steps can be taken to help the client achieve a particular set of goals or objectives. The International Coaching Federation defines coaching as “partnering with clients in a thought-provoking and creative process that inspires them to maximize their personal and professional potential”.

 Although there are many different types of coaches—leadership, executive, life, wellness, and career—all coaches engage with their clients in thought-provoking, creative processes in order to unlock their clients’ potential and impact the outlook each client has on a specific aspect of their work or personal life.

Career coach

 Career coaching differs from leadership, executive, life, and wellness coaching in several respects. The objective of Career coaching is to support clients in making informed decisions about their career development and trajectory, including assisting with a transition to an encore career. Through career coaching, Prystins Insights Coaching can offer various tools and strategies for leaders to use in meeting their goals. These would include the development of resumes, cover letters, and curriculum vitae, interview preparation, employment contract and severance agreement negotiations, using social media, networking, career mapping, and polishing your leadership brand. The career coaching agenda typically is a combination of both professional and life components, with success measured by the extent to which Prystins is able to help sort out issues related to the client’s professional future. A successful career coaching engagement also results in the client gaining career confidence, understanding insights gained from assessments such as Edgar Schein’s Career Anchors, along with receiving encouragement and inspiration from our coaches.

Who needs a career coach?

 Career coaching can be helpful at every point of a professional path. Working with a good career coach in the early years of one’s career can put individuals on more solid footing—not just with a well-crafted resume and strategies to broaden one’s network, but also with the addition of an actionable leadership-career management plan. Consulting with us can even be extremely valuable when trying to determine the best type of graduate degree program to complete in order to help further one’s career goals. It can be confusing and daunting for professionals to decipher and understand the myriad of features, benefits, and options available in graduate school. Professionals to be often express frustration with how little formal guidance they ever received on this important (and costly) investment in their career. Often the first step includes a self-assessment of the leadership competencies required in a new role or setting. Having a clearer understanding of how your current competencies can be transferred to a different setting is important information to emphasize, for example in an interview.

 Awareness of one’s own competencies through self- assessment , can provide the coach and client with a new job search strategy framework that can be used to evaluate potential positions, and to develop cover letters, objective statements, and targeted resumes.

Who benefits the most from career coaching?

 The candidate who will derive the greater benefit from our coaching is someone who is open to new ideas, is willing to step out of his or her comfort zone, is motivated to embark on the challenge of thinking differently about job search and career change process, and is willing to take risks will derive the greatest benefit. How far out on the fringes one can go partly depends on how much risk one is willing or able to take.

 Professionals should reach out to their career coach for as much guidance as possible, as well as for help from anyone within their personal or professional networks, in order to succeed.

 If you would like to explore how professional career coaching/counseling can benefit you, we invite you to read further to learn more about our career services and our complimentary career services consultation.

Find the Work You Were Designed to Do

  At Prystins insights we offer affordable one-on-one career counseling/coaching services that can help you discover and obtain meaningful, fulfilling work that fits your God-given design. Professional career services can help you to:

  • Clarify your God-given design through professional career testing;
  • Identify the work you were designed to do;
  • Develop short- and long-term career goals for living your calling;
  • Discern God's will for your career;
  • Land a new or better job;
  • Negotiate a salary that represents the true value you bring to a job;
  • Gain the competitive edge with a high impact resume and other marketing materials and strategies;
  • Prove that you are the person to hire by utilizing the best job search strategies for today's job market.

The purpose of the consultation is to provide you with a professional career analysis and determine which career program or service will best meet your specific needs and goals. (If we do not feel that we can meet your needs and goals, we will let you know. We meet one-on-one with prospective clients to make sure that there is a good fit between the client's needs and what can be accomplished through our services.)

  What happens during the career services consultation? You will have a one-on-one sessions During your session, you will have the opportunity to discuss your career situation with the career coach, clarify your goals for career coaching, learn more about the career programs or services of Prystins Insights that would best meet your specific needs, and determine which career program or service will fit you best.
